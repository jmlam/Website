Customer Orders Data¶

  • "Customer": Unique customers who started buying our products in Jan or February 2018
  • "Date Shipped": Date when orders were fulfilled. Note that some customers made multiple orders (repeat purchases) while other customers did not.
  • "Product ID": Unique SKU representing which product was ordered
  • "Brand": The customers were buyers of one of 3 brands. They only purchased from 1 of the 3.
  • "Channel": How we acquired these customers

The existance of surge days of relatively similar scale are also confirmed by the exceedingly similar amount of skewness between each brand. Skew greater than zero suggests weight in the right tail, meaning there are number of observerations that are inflating the mean amount of sales per day. It can be concluded, due to similar skew, that all brands experience surges, and at about the same amount relative to the mean sales per day.

However, as noted by the graph above, although the variation and skewness of each brand is similar, the scale is not. This is most apparent in the fourth graph, where each Brands mean is graphed against each other. By far, White Whale has a much higher mean sales per day than either of the other brands. Previously it was supposed that the White Whale sales was grossly inflated by a particular point in time. Yet, it is visibile that all brands experience some form of inflation, but, due to the similar degree of inflation, can still be compared. Since this is the case, White Whale's mean can be seen as more legitimate and further solidifies its posistion as the preferred brand amongst repeat customers.

Conclusion:¶

It would seem that the White Whale Brand is the most popular brand amongst repeat customers both across the customer base and intertemporally. When testing the idea that a small subset of repeat customers may be pushing a large number of White Whale orders, thus inflating sales, the proportion of the White Whale Brand customers remained unchanged even after filtering only for unique repeat customers. When testing the idea that a surge purchase of White Whale orders at one point in time had inflated the sales, it was found that such surges do exist, but across all brands. Overall, White Whale seems to be the preferred brand of a repeat customer.

Conclusion¶

As stated before, the most effective method of customer acquistion amongst repeat customers is through a Direct Channel. Whether it be in a less popular brand such as Black Dog or Orange Cat, or within a more popular brand such as White Whale, repeat customers who ordered goods were ultimately acquired through a Direct channel. Conclusion¶

Looking at each brand through the perspective of individual product sales greatly expanded the insight gained from the previous analysis of repeat customer preferred brand. The particular preference towards White Whale was not actually attributed to the brand as a whole, but rather to roughly 4 products (17, 548, 581, and 1313) that made up the vast majority of the brands sales. A similar phenomenon was observed in the Black Dog brand, with a few specific products making up the majority of the brand sales, but not as extreme. When these top performers are excluded, the remaining products have a remarkbly similar performance accross the brands, although Black Dog still demonstrates a noticeable dropoff. However, such a graph is more related to topic of which products are unwanted by repeat customers rather than preferred.

In terms of top preferred products, not all brands demonstrate an absolute disparity like the White Whale brand and a minimum cutoff point would have to be artificially set. In this case study, a 15% portion of all brand sales will be the arbitrarily set minimum cuttoff for product preference based on a visual approximation of each brand.

In this sample of repeat customers, given a product commands at least 15% of its respective brand sales, the top performing products per brand are as follows:

  • Black Dog Products: 395, 494, 239, and 1073
  • Orange Cat Products: 1202, 1145, 1415, and 1304
  • White Whale Products: 17, 548, and 581

Reccomendations¶

In all, this sample and subsequent analysis have shown that among repeat customers the White Whale brand is by far the most successful. However, this success is due to a select few products which have grossly inflated the brand's presence in the sample, while the rest of products within the brand have had a mediocre performance. Moreover, within the White Whale brand, the top two products show a continued projection for success while the third ranked product seems to have stagnated. As a whole, the White Whale brand is likely carried by its top products, and though it would be beneficial to invest heavily in the brand, it would hasty to invest exclusively.

Amongst the next two brands, the Black Dog brand shows much more promise than the Orange Cat brand. Not only has it had more success overall, but its products have shown more recent success that the Orange Cat brand simply has not contested. The Black Dog brand, however, contains the most products out of all the brands, and as a whole demonstrates very tapered sales; most likely, this brand could use more specific reworking of its products, trimming those that have obviously underperformed.

In total a broad overview of reccomendations from this analysis are as follows:

  • Invest heavily in the White Whale brand, particularily in the top 2 products, and reconsider the products outside the top 4.
  • Invest modestly in the Black Dog brand, particularly in the 2nd ranked product, and consider trimming the brand of underperforming products.
  • Invest minimally in the Orange Cat brand.
  • Invest heavily in direct channels of customer acquisition (covered in respective section).

Conclusion¶

As a whole the profile of a repeat customer seems to be heavily inclined towards the top products of the White Whale Brand with the strong majority of being acquired through a Direct Channel.

Caveat¶

Even though these are the reccomendations from this analysis, there are still a number of factors that are simply not avaliable in the dataset. Valuable information such as region, product type, customer satisfaction, amongst others, could provide many other insights. For instance, although the Orange Cat Brand does quite poorly in these overall sales, it could be the favorite of a region and removing the brand entirely could resulting in losing the company's presence in that region.

Also, as briefly noted above, these reccomendations are broad insights from the dataset, as the scope of this analysis was limited to profiling the preferences of a repeat customer. Any major product decisions likely neccessitate a brand specific investigation/analysis.
